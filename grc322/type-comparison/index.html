History

Baskerville

The typeface Baskerville was created in the 1750s by John Baskerville. Baskerville was a very wealthy industrialist in Birmingham, England, where the typeface was created. The typeface was cut into metal by John Handy, a punchcutter. The main reason behind this typeface being made was a part of an ambitious project to create books of the greatest possible quality. Because of this, Baskerville became very popular in book design. Also, Baskerville was intended as a refinement of old-styler typefaces from the creation period.

Caslon

The typeface Caslon was created in 1722 by William Caslon. Caslon worked as an engraver of punches London, England, being where he created the typeface. Caslon was created as the first original English typeface. Caslon types were distributed throughout the British Empire and British North America. With that being said, Caslon was used in the U.S. Declaration of Independence. Many years after creation, Caslon continues to be a standard in typography today.
